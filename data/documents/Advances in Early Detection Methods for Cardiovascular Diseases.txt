Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) remain a leading cause of mortality globally, prompting extensive research into early detection methods that can improve patient outcomes through timely intervention. 
Recent advances have focused on the integration of biomarkers, imaging technologies, and machine learning algorithms to identify risk factors before clinical symptoms become severe. 
For example, blood-based biomarkers such as high-sensitivity C-reactive protein and troponins are increasingly used to detect inflammation and cardiac stress, while imaging techniques like echocardiography and CT angiography provide detailed visualization of heart structures. 
In addition, predictive models that analyze large datasets from electronic health records can identify individuals at high risk based on patterns in lifestyle, genetics, and medical history. 
These approaches enable more personalized treatment strategies and preventive care. However, challenges remain in ensuring the affordability, accessibility, and accuracy of these technologies across different populations. 
Ongoing research continues to refine these tools, aiming to balance early detection with cost-effectiveness and broad applicability in healthcare systems.
