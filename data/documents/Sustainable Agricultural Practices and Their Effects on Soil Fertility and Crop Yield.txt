Sustainable agriculture focuses on farming methods that maintain environmental health while ensuring long-term productivity, with particular emphasis on soil conservation, water management, and biodiversity. 
Research in this field has shown that practices such as crop rotation, organic fertilization, reduced tillage, and integrated pest management can significantly improve soil structure and nutrient content over time. 
These methods help prevent soil degradation, reduce dependency on chemical inputs, and enhance the resilience of farming systems to climate variability. 
Studies comparing conventional and sustainable systems often find that while initial yields in sustainable systems may be slightly lower, they tend to stabilize and sometimes surpass conventional yields in the long term, especially under stress conditions like drought. 
Furthermore, sustainable practices contribute to reduced environmental impact by minimizing pollution and preserving ecosystems. 
Despite these advantages, adoption remains uneven due to factors such as economic constraints, lack of technical knowledge, and policy limitations. 
Continued research aims to optimize these practices and make them more accessible to farmers worldwide.
