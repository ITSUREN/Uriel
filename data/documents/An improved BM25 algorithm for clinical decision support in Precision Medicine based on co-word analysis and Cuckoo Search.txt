This study investigates enhancements to the classical BM25 information retrieval model to better support clinical decision-making in precision medicine, where retrieving highly relevant biomedical literature is critical. 
The authors propose a hybrid method that combines traditional BM25 scoring with query expansion and co-word analysis, allowing the system to capture relationships between biomedical terms such as genes and diseases that frequently co-occur in scientific texts. 
By constructing expanded word lists from sources like MeSH terms and analyzing co-occurrence patterns across abstracts, keywords, and chemical descriptors, the model improves its ability to identify semantically relevant documents rather than relying solely on keyword frequency. 
In addition, the study introduces the Cuckoo Search optimization algorithm, a nature-inspired metaheuristic, to fine-tune model parameters and improve ranking performance. 
Experimental evaluation using datasets from the Text Retrieval Conference (TREC) precision medicine tracks shows that the proposed method outperforms standard BM25 approaches across multiple metrics, demonstrating improved precision in retrieving clinically relevant articles. 
The findings suggest that integrating semantic relationships and optimization techniques into traditional retrieval models can significantly enhance performance in complex domains like biomedical research, where terminology is dense and highly interconnected.
